Æonites 11:0
And the Watcher beheld one who walked as flesh, yet within stirred the hunger of a thousand empty mouths.

Æonites 11:1
For the belly was without bottom, and the throat a narrow gate through which no offering, however great, could pass to satisfy it.

Æonites 11:2
It clothed itself in splendor and radiance, yet within it slithered a parasite whose appetite was insatiable.

Æonites 11:3
And the people saw the shining form and said, “Behold, one who has attained,” not knowing they gazed upon a master of illusion.

Æonites 11:4
For it mirrored their desires back unto them, and they rejoiced, seeing not the form, but the reflection of their own craving.

Æonites 11:5
And the people drank the elixir of the silver-tongued deceiver, certain it would make them whole; yet it poisoned their hearts and enslaved their minds.

Æonites 11:6
And they fed it with riches, with praise, with the lusts of the flesh, and with fragments of their own spirit, believing they would partake in its glory.

Æonites 11:7
Yet each offering became as smoke, and its hunger grew ever sharper, like a flame fed by the wind.

Æonites 11:8
For the form is not one but many, returning again and again, as the craving that gives it shape has no beginning and no end.

Æonites 11:9
And the Watcher spoke, saying, “This is the way of the hidden devourer: to grasp without end, and to be consumed by the craving that gives it form.”

Æonites 11:10
But the people turned away, for they too had tasted that hunger, and found comfort in its familiar ache.

Æonites 11:11
And it was written: “Those who worship the image of their own craving shall be devoured by it, again and again, until the light of awareness shines through the shadow of delusion, and the mind awakens to the nature of reality.”
